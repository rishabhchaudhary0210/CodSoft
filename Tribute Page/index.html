Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

About

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, fondly known as the "People's President," was a remarkable Indian scientist and politician who left an indelible mark on the nation's scientific and political landscape. He was born on October 15, 1931, in the small town of Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. From humble beginnings, Dr. Kalam went on to become one of the most revered figures in Indian history.

Dr. Kalam's journey began with a degree in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology, after which he joined the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). His brilliance and dedication were soon evident, and he played a pivotal role in India's first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV) program. This achievement marked the beginning of his illustrious career in space research and technology.

One of his most significant contributions was to India's defense technology. Dr. Kalam was the driving force behind the development of India's first indigenous ballistic missile, the Agni, which catapulted India into an elite group of nations with intercontinental ballistic missile capabilities. His leadership in the development of the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (IGMDP) earned him the title of the "Missile Man of India."

Besides his scientific prowess, Dr. Kalam was a visionary educator. He recognized the importance of nurturing young minds and inspiring them to pursue science and technology. Throughout his life, he interacted with countless students, encouraging them to dream big and work hard. His autobiography, "Wings of Fire," became a source of inspiration for millions of young Indians.

Dr. Kalam's contributions extended beyond the realms of science and technology. In 2002, he took on the role of the President of India, serving a five-year term. During his tenure, he continued to advocate for education and innovation, often visiting schools and colleges to connect with the youth. His presidency was marked by his simplicity, humility, and unwavering commitment to the welfare of the people.

Even after his presidential term, Dr. Kalam remained an active participant in India's educational and scientific endeavors. He received numerous awards and honors, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award. His dedication to the nation and his vision for a self-reliant India in science and technology continue to inspire generations.

Tragically, Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while doing what he loved the most - interacting with students.While delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong, Kalam collapsed and died from an apparent cardiac arrest at the age of 83 years. His legacy lives on, not only in the numerous achievements and milestones he accomplished but also in the hearts of millions who continue to be inspired by his life, values, and teachings.

Major Achievements

  • Lead scientist in India's first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV).
  • Played a key role in the development of India's first indigenous ballistic missile, the Agni.
  • Authored several books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds," inspiring millions of young minds.

Honours

  • In 1997, Kalam received India's highest civilian honour, the Bharat Ratna, for his contribution to the scientific research and modernisation of defence technology in India.
  • In 2013, he was the recipient of the Von Braun Award from the National Space Society "to recognize excellence in the management and leadership of a space-related project".
  • Wheeler Island, a national missile test site in Odisha, was renamed Kalam Island in September 2015.
  • A prominent road in New Delhi was renamed from Aurangzeb Road to Dr APJ Abdul Kalam Road in August 2015.
  • AIn February 2018, scientists from the Botanical Survey of India named a newly found plant species as Drypetes kalamii, in his honour.
