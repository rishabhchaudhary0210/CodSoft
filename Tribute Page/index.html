About Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, fondly known as the "People's President," was a remarkable Indian scientist and politician who left an indelible mark on the nation's scientific and political landscape. He was born on October 15, 1931, in the small town of Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. From humble beginnings, Dr. Kalam went on to become one of the most revered figures in Indian history.

Major Achievements

  • Lead scientist in India's first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV).
  • Played a key role in the development of India's first indigenous ballistic missile, the Agni.
  • Authored several books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds," inspiring millions of young minds.
